Course Content

The Department of Information Management provides a comprehensive curriculum covering fields such as data science, network security, and artificial intelligence, aiming to develop students' abilities in problem analysis and problem-solving.

Required Courses

The following are freshman required courses, designed to build a foundational understanding of information management:

Computational Programming

Course Description: Introduces the basic concepts of computational programming, including fundamental principles of computation, basic knowledge of data structures and algorithms, and approaches to effective programming and solving computational problems.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (2/0)

Freshman English

Course Description: Provides basic English proficiency training, emphasizing integrated practice in listening, speaking, reading, and writing to help students enhance their English skills for daily communication and academic research.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (2/2)

Physical Education

Course Description: Aims to improve students' physical fitness and health through practical sports activities, fostering teamwork and overall well-being, and encouraging lifelong exercise habits.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (2/2)

Management

Course Description: Introduces basic principles and methods of management, exploring organizational structure, decision-making, leadership, planning, and control processes to build a foundation in business management.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (3/0)

Introduction to Computers

Course Description: Provides foundational knowledge of computers, including principles of computer composition, hardware architecture, and operating systems, helping students understand the application and development of computers in modern society.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (3/0)

Programming

Course Description: Teaches basic programming knowledge, including the use of programming languages for logical operations and data processing, laying the groundwork for advanced courses.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (3/3)

Exploring Advanced Information

Course Description: Introduces cutting-edge technologies in the information field, including artificial intelligence, big data, and blockchain, along with their future development trends.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (2/0)

Multimedia Literacy

Course Description: Covers information processing and interpretation skills in the multimedia era, teaching students to analyze various media contents like text, images, and audio, and fostering critical thinking.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (0/2)

Probability and Statistics

Course Description: Introduces fundamental concepts in probability and statistics, including random events, probability distributions, and statistical inference, and teaches how to apply these methods to solve practical and professional problems.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (0/3)

Elective Courses

Elective courses provide in-depth knowledge in specialized fields and enhance practical application skills:

Programming Practice

Course Description: Offers practical programming and implementation experience, where students learn to write, test, and optimize programs, working on small projects to develop hands-on skills.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (2/2)

Introduction to the Metaverse

Course Description: Introduces the fundamental concepts and technologies of the metaverse, exploring how virtual reality and augmented reality are used to create immersive digital worlds and their potential in future society and industries.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (3/0)

Calculus

Course Description: Covers the basics of calculus, including limits, derivatives, integrals, and their real-world applications, providing a foundation in mathematical analysis and modeling.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (0/3)

Multimedia Software Applications

Course Description: Teaches the use of various multimedia software tools, enabling students to create and edit media content such as images, audio, and video.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (0/3)

Multimedia Color Technology and Applications

Course Description: Introduces color theory and its applications in digital media, teaching students about digital color representation, color-matching techniques, and using color to enhance visual design.

(Fall/Spring) Credits: (0/3)

Course Features

The courses in this department cover two major fields: "Information" and "Management". Emphasizing a balance between theory and practice, key subjects include programming, information software development, enterprise information applications, business management, network applications, and information security management. Graduates from this department excel in both the workplace and further education.
In order to improve the quality and depth of teaching and research, the department has established multiple research centers and built up a rich set of facilities, striving to combine teaching with real-world practice. The department also provides various internship opportunities, allowing students to gain hands-on experience in the workplace before graduation, identify their strengths and weaknesses, and better appreciate the learning opportunities.
The department's key course, "Graduation Project", requires students to work in teams to complete a full system project before graduation to develop practical skills. Every year, the department holds a graduation exhibition and party, attracting industry and academic leaders for visits, and receiving much praise. Students often win top prizes in national competitions, which not only enhances the department's reputation but also provides excellent career opportunities for graduates.

Curriculum Structure by Group

The curriculum design of this department focuses on the combination of practical operations and theoretical learning. Students will not only acquire the basic knowledge of information technology but also gain in-depth knowledge in specialized fields such as database management, artificial intelligence, big data processing, and enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems. We expect students to master these technologies through practice and use them to solve various challenges in business management.

Information Management Group Curriculum

The Information Management group incorporates the unique features of Shih Hsin University, with an emphasis on data business communication and media technology applications.
The focus of the Information Management group is on big data analysis, cloud-based smart enterprises, and enterprise resource planning (ERP), using the latest information technologies to assist businesses in their digital transformation and innovation. This approach strengthens understanding of the information system needs of businesses, moving to business decision-making and communication based on data.
As cloud services become a major trend, the Information Management group strengthens its curriculum to equip students with skills in hardware and software deployment and application in cloud environments. Hundreds of students have passed international cloud certification exams.
Although the Information Management group differs from the other two groups, students can still take cross-group courses, such as artificial intelligence, smart IoT, blockchain, metaverse, and information security.
In the innovative computer classrooms, students learn through hands-on practice, developing the ability to create information-related works. Internship courses are also arranged to help students successfully enter the workforce.

Computer Science Group Curriculum

The Computer Science group’s curriculum includes programming, information security, database systems, and app development (Android/iOS). Additionally, it covers cutting-edge technologies such as artificial intelligence. Advanced courses in the Computer Science group include game development, big data, computer graphics, image processing, computer vision, and deep learning. Students can explore and learn various areas within the information field.
These are some of the hottest fields in the information industry today, and the learning focus is on practical skills and applications, which can be directly applied in the workplace.
The information field has always been highly sought after in the job market. Graduates often pursue careers as software engineers, database administrators, algorithm engineers, information security managers, AI research and development engineers, and more. With the ongoing acceleration of digital transformation, all industries require information technology professionals. According to surveys of graduate destinations, graduates with IT expertise are able to quickly find higher-paying jobs. Overall, the Computer Science group’s curriculum covers a wide range of information technology skills, allowing students to explore and find their ideal career paths.

Smart Network Group Curriculum

In today's digital life, both individuals and businesses are inseparable from networks and cloud services. Having relevant expertise guarantees employment. The Smart Network Group of the Information Management Department at Shih Hsin University offers basic network courses, network programming courses, and professional certification courses, as well as specialized courses in smart IoT and cloud services, such as Introduction to Artificial Intelligence, Smart Networking, Network Security, Cloud Architecture, and Cloud Computing. These courses train next-generation professionals with solid foundation and immediate job readiness.
The Smart Network Group provides a structured international certification curriculum, such as CISCO CCNA, RHCSA, and Amazon Cloud certifications. Graduates often work as network systems engineers, network application developers, network planners and managers, network security analysts, cloud engineers, and cloud architects. Alumni work in major information companies and large banks in Taiwan, such as Foxconn, ASUS, Acer, Wistron, China Trust Bank, Changhua Bank, and Yongfeng Bank.
